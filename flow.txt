System Flow
HOW SYSTEM WORKS (SHORT & CLEAR)
🟢 Purchase
create transaction
create purchase
create account_entries:
cash/bank → credit
supplier → credit (if unpaid)
create product_histories (+qty)
🔵 Sale
create transaction
create sale
create account_entries:
cash/bank → debit
customer → debit (if unpaid)
create product_histories (-qty)
💸 Expense
create transaction
create expense
create account_entries:
expense → debit
cash/bank → credit

balance_type indicates the natural behavior of an account:

Debit-type account → normally increases when money comes in
Credit-type account → normally increases when money goes out

⚖️ How to assign balance_type for supplier/customer
Account Type	Balance Type	Explanation
Supplier	credit	Supplier is someone you owe money to. When you purchase, your debt increases → credit. When you pay them, your debt decreases → debit.
Customer	debit	Customer is someone who owes you money. When you sell, their owed amount increases → debit. When they pay, it decreases → credit.
Cash / Bank	debit	Money you have → debit-type
Expense	debit	Spending → debit-type


Stick to this everywhere:

Money coming in → debit cash
Money going out → credit cash
You owe someone → credit (supplier)
Someone owes you → debit (customer)
Expense → always debit
